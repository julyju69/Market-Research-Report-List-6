<p><p>The fire-resistant cable material market comprises several key types, each offering unique properties. XLPE (cross-linked polyethylene) provides excellent thermal and chemical resistance, making it suitable for high-temperature environments. LSZH (low smoke zero halogen) cables minimize toxic smoke emissions, enhancing safety in enclosed spaces. PVC (polyvinyl chloride) is cost-effective and versatile, commonly used in various applications, though it produces smoke when burned. EPR (ethylene propylene rubber) offers flexibility and resilience, ideal for harsh weather conditions, making it effective for fire-resistant cable applications.</p></p>
