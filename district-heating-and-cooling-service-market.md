<p><p>District Heating and Cooling Service refers to a centralized system that distributes thermal energy for heating or cooling to multiple buildings or facilities within a defined area. District Heating involves generating hot water or steam at a centralized plant, which is then distributed through insulated pipes. Conversely, District Cooling produces chilled water from a central plant, supplying it to buildings for air conditioning. Both systems enhance energy efficiency, reduce emissions, and support sustainable urban development by optimizing resource use.</p></p>
