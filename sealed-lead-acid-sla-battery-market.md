<p><p>Sealed Lead Acid (SLA) batteries are rechargeable energy storage systems that use lead dioxide as the positive plate and sponge lead as the negative plate, with sulfuric acid as the electrolyte. They are tightly sealed, preventing electrolyte leakage, making them suitable for various applications including emergency lighting, uninterruptible power supplies (UPS), and electric vehicles. <p><p>The Sealed Lead Acid (SLA) battery market primarily comprises two types: Absorbent Glass Mat (AGM) and Gel batteries. AGM batteries utilize fiberglass mats to absorb electrolyte, providing a robust option with high discharge rates, ideal for applications like solar energy and uninterruptible power supplies. Gel batteries, on the other hand, consist of a silica-based gel that immobilizes the electrolyte, making them spill-proof and particularly suited for deep cycling applications in marine and RV use. Both types offer reliable power solutions with distinct advantages.</p></p>
